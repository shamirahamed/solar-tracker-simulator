About

About this tool

This simulator models a single-axis solar tracker system using location, date, panel geometry, row spacing, and operating mode. It combines summary KPIs, charts, and a 2D engineering-style visual to compare tracker behavior with and without backtracking.

Calculations included

  • Solar position from latitude, longitude, date, and timezone
  • Ideal tracker angle
  • Limited tracker angle using max rotation limit
  • Backtracking angle
  • Shadow length
  • Inter-row shading indication
  • Ground Coverage Ratio (GCR)
  • Power output and daily energy comparison
  • Sunrise and sunset extraction

Calculation formulas

Ground Coverage Ratio (GCR) GCR = panel width / row spacing
Limited tracker angle θ_limited = clamp(θ_tracker, -θ_max, +θ_max)
Shadow length Shadow Length = Height / tan(Solar Elevation)
Effective shadow Effective Shadow = max(0, Shadow Length − Row Spacing)
Shading % Shading % = min(100, (Effective Shadow / Panel Width) × 100)
Power Power = Irradiance × Area × Efficiency
Daily energy Energy = Σ Power(t) × Δt
Energy gain % Gain % = (With BT − Without BT) / Without BT × 100
Sunrise / Sunset Elevation crossing above or below 0°
Backtracking θ_backtracking = f(GCR, solar elevation, geometry)

Assumptions

  • 2D visualization may be compressed to fit smaller screens
  • Displayed numeric values come from simulation results
  • Visual panel spacing may be compressed, but relative shadow-to-spacing scale is preserved
  • Ground shadow may be visible even when inter-row shading is 0%, if the shadow does not reach the next row.
  • This is a practical engineering dashboard, not a full PVsyst replacement

References & Disclaimer

This simulator is based on standard solar geometry and tracking concepts inspired by open-source libraries such as pvlib and general photovoltaic system modeling practices.

  • Solar position calculations are conceptually based on pvlib methodologies.
  • Backtracking logic is derived from standard single-axis tracker principles using Ground Coverage Ratio (GCR) and sun position relationships.
  • Shadow length and inter-row shading are estimated using simplified geometric relationships.
  • Power calculations are based on simplified irradiance × area × efficiency assumptions.

Important Notes

  • This tool is intended for visualization and conceptual understanding only.
  • Results may not exactly match real-world tracker systems due to mechanical constraints, terrain slope, OEM controller differences, weather variability, and loss assumptions.
  • Backtracking in this model is an approximation and may differ from commercial tools such as PVsyst or real tracker controller logic.
  • Inter-row shading is calculated using geometric assumptions and does not include electrical mismatch or detailed shading loss modeling.
